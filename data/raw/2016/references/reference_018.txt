Binay accusers in Makati building case file new petition; Nancy says she won't use proxies
By:
InterAksyon.com
August 18, 2014 5:03 PM
Vice President Jejomar Binay in file photo with children Erwin "Junjun" who is Makati mayor, Rep. Abigail Binay-Campos and Sen. Nancy Binay. The vice president and the mayor are accused of plunder in the allegedly overpriced Makati carpark building. Senator Binay said she won't participate in the Senate blue ribbon hearing on the case.
Tweet
InterAksyon.com
The online news portal of TV5
MANILA, Philippines – The accusers of Vice President Jejomar Binay and his son, Makati Mayor Junjun Binay in the alleged overprice of a carpark building filed Monday a supplemental petition reflecting a bigger cost for the project. This, as Senator Nancy Binay said she will not attend the Senate blue ribbon hearing on the case, nor will use her peers in the minority as proxies to throw questions.
Complainants Renato Bondal and Nicolas Enciso IV beefed up their plunder case against the Binays and 23 other officials, raising the alleged overprice to the Makati parking building to P1,913,366,502.50.
In a nine-page supplement to their original complaint submitted to the Office of the Ombudsman, Bondal and Enciso VI told the Office of the Ombudsman they filed the supplement based on the report by Cecilia Caga-anan, supervising auditor of the Commission on Audit.
The latter supposedly admitted in her memorandum to Director Carmelita O. Antasuda of the COA Local Government Sector in the National Capital Region that 10 ordinances were approved appropriating a total of P2,711,566,502.50 for the 11-storey Makati parking building starting in 2007, when Vice President Binay was still Makati mayor.
Bondal and Enciso said the revelations of auditor Caga-anan increased the overprice for the 11-storey structure, which had six floors exclusively for parking and five floors for offices.
In earlier interviews, the vice president and the mayor said it was misleading to characterize the building as simply a carpark building. It is an office building that incorporates features of a modern, elegant but "green" building, they said. They also pointed to the clearance given by the COA for the project.
Caga-anan has been included as one of the 24 respondents in the plunder case along with Binay, his son and current Mayor Erwin Jejomar S. Binay Jr., and 21 former or current city councilors.
Senate blue ribbon hearing Thursday
In an interview after the Senate Blue Ribbon sub-committee members’ meeting on Monday, Sen. Nancy Binay said she will not make any "noise" during the hearing on Senate Proposed Resolution No. 826 filed by Senator Antonio Trillanes IV on the building.
“Definitely, I will not ask questions. Pero ang inisip ko pa nga 'yan kung gusto kong manood eh, pero hindi ko nga alam kung puwede doon lang ako sa gallery [But what I have in mind is that I still wish to watch the proceedings. I just don't know if I can stay at the gallery],” Binay said.
She said it was unseemly of her to ask questions of the complainants, as “it would look like I am using my position para pabanguhin lang kapatid ko [to make my broether come out smelling clean] and I think he can very well answer the issue,” Binay added.
Binay also rejected the notion of passing on her questions to acting Senate Minority Leader Vicente Sotto III or Senator JV Ejercito, to clarify things. “No, no, I won't give phone-in questions,” Binay said.
She said Sotto and Ejercito are competent enough to ask the right questions, in order to ferret out the truth. "So, there is no need for me to give questions. She added that, based on the "interview of my brother earlier today, he answered the issues point by point.”
Besides, Binay said she does not want to be accused of lawyering for her brother or father.
“As you know, the Senate is heavily criticized as it is for lawyering for certain individuals. I don't want to be accused of lawyering for my brother or my father. If possible, I just want to stay in the gallery,” Binay said.
Still, Binay noted that instead of wasting public money on an investigation which was reported out by the Commission on Audit (COA) and is now pending with the Ombudsman, the Senate should focus on more important legislative duties.
“It's just a pity because there are more important thing to do than  tackle this issue. The senators should exert an effort to get a copy of the COA report instead of wasting tax payers’ money on the hearing,” Binay said.
The Senate Blue Ribbon Committee hearing on Thursday will be presided over by Senator Aquilino “Koko” Pimentel III.
