VIDEO | VP Binay files P200-M damage suit over 'orchestrated' smear drive to derail 2016 candidacy
By:
Ernie Reyes, InterAksyon.com | Jove Francisco, News5
July 20, 2015 11:57 AM
Tweet
InterAksyon.com
The online news portal of TV5
MANILA - (UPDATE 3, 8:36 a.m., July 21, 2015) Vice President Jejomar Binay on Monday filed a P200-million damage suit against Senators Alan Peter Cayetano and Antonio Trillanes, ex-vice mayor Ernesto Mercado, and members of the Anti-Money Laundering Council, for what he called a "well-organized and orchestrated effort" to destroy his reputation and wipe out his chances to be elected president.
"This shameful exercise is prohibited by law, particularly under Articles 19, 20 and 21 (human relations), 32 (constitutional rights), 33 (civil aspect of libel) and 2176 (quasi-delict) of the Civil Code," Binay said, in filing the report that cited the damage caused to him by among others, the 11-month open-ended Senate hearings spearheaded by two senators; and the publication of a report on the freeze of his bank accounts and those of people linked to him.
Other defendants in the case are Ombudsman Conchita Carpio-Morales, Caloocan City Rep. Eric Erice, Atty. Renato Bondal, Engr. Mario Hechanova, AMLC officials and the
Philippine Daily Inquirer
.
Also charged were Bangko Sentral governor Amando M. Tetangco Jr. chairman of AMLC, and the council members: insurance commissioner Emmanuel F. Dooc, SEC chair Teresita J. Herbosa, and AMLC executive director Julia C. Abad.
The complaint was filed Monday morning by Binay at the Makati City Prosecutor's Office.
The complaint arose from, among others, the continuing Senate hearings that have lasted nearly a year; several publications of supposed "investigative" stories in the PDI; various media interviews by "talking heads" like Erice, and the publication of an AMLC report submitted to the Court of Appeals, listing 242 bank accounts that the court froze. Of these accounts, VP Binay's camp said in earlier interviews he owned only five.
Businessmen and associates listed in the freeze order included Antonio Tiu of Sunchamp, his brother and sister-in-law and mother. Senator Trillanes and Mercado had called Tiu a "dummy" of VP Binay.
Binay tagged Mercado and Bondal among others as the Mercado Group; Trillanes, Cayetano and Erice as the designated “talking heads,” and the AMLC officers as “government inquisitors.”
The 'Modus Operandi'
Binay said the primaty objective of the mission is to destroy his image and reputation.
“The Defendants used the principle espoused by the infamous Nazi propagandist, Joseph Gobbels – 'If you tell a lie big enough and keep repeating it, people will eventually come to believe it.' The campaign began from what he called the Mercado Group, the political enemies of his family, who came up with what he described as the most incredulous accusations against the Vice President but are bereft of any legal and/or factual basis.
Among the accusations made by the Mercado Group were: the alleged overpricing in the Makati City Hall Building II and the Makati Science High School Building and cakes for the elderly; fixing of permits and tax relief in exchange for a unit in every condominium; Binay's alleged ownership of the 350-hectare property in Rosario, Batangas; 13% kickbacks from every city project; and an alleged P200-million kickback from the sale of the Boy Scout of the Philippines (BSP) property;
“All told, the accusations made by the Mercado Group are just that, mere accusations. They are, however, sufficient for Defendants’ purposes – they have the lies, these lies just have to be repeated long and consistently enough to create a negative public perception on the Vice President,” Binay said.
Senate hearings
Binay said the Mercado Group connived with the other defendants, using the halls of the Senate as the public venue for them to accuse the Vice President of all manner of crimes regardless of the absence of proof, .
“For their efforts, the Mercado Group was immediately rewarded with coverage under the Witness Protection Program to protect them from any liability for their lies. So far, the Senate Investigation is in its 22nd hearing, the longest ever recorded in the annals of the Senate,” Binay said.
Thereafter, Binay said Trillanes,   Cayetano and Erice, as the designated “talking heads”, repeated the lies of the Mercado Group in media interviews in order to ensure maximum repetition.
Binay tagged the
Philippine Daily Inquirer
as the “biased media outlet” spouting lies dished out by the Mercado Group in the halls of the Senate through screaming headlines and “news” articles that are misleading and/or maliciously slanted in order to create a public perception that the Vice President is corrupt and unworthy of being elected President.
In 44 issues from October 8 to November 20, 2014, the Binay camp counted:
“Defendant Inquirer had 29 banner-stories on the allegations against the Vice President. The prominence given by Defendant Inquirer to the allegations against the Vice President was precisely calculated to have maximum impact in the survey that was to be conducted by Pulse Asia on November 14 to 20, 2014.”
'General fishing expedition'
Meanwhile, Binay said the AMLC group allegedly undertook a general fishing expedition using government agencies and resources against him. “These individuals provided the veneer of legality to the lies peddled by the Mercado Group,” Binay said.
“Instead of approaching the accusations against the Vice President with the cold impartiality as required by their official functions, Defendants Tetangco, Dooc, Herbosa and Abad maliciously ignored official public documents (ITRs and Statement of Campaign Contributions and Expenses duly file with the BIR and the COMELEC for the last 30 years) and slanted their report to make it appear that the Vice President is guilty of money laundering. More, said officials tolerated if not instigated the premature release of the AMLC report to Defendant Inquirer who published the same repeatedly in clear violation of Section 9 (c) of the Anti-Money Laundering Act,” Binay said.
On the other hand, the vice president said that Morales exhibited a bellicose attitude against him.
“She took an extraordinary interest in the prosecution of the Vice President to the extent that: (a) she chose to defy a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) issued by the Court of Appeals stopping her from suspending the son of the Vice President as Mayor of Makati; (b) she voluntarily initiated, appeared and argued a petition before the Supreme Court to question the TRO issued by the Court of Appeals notwithstanding the fact that this is the role of the Office of the Solicitor General; and (c) she refused to apply the principle of “condonation” to exculpate the Vice President’s son from administrative liability but applied the very same doctrine to other respondents before her office. And, in concert with other Defendants, Defendant Carpio-Morales saw to it that she repeatedly tells the media that the evidence of corruption against the Vice President is strong,” Binay said.
Trillanes, Erice ready to face suit
In separate statements, Senator Antonio Trillanes IV and Caloocan City Rep. Edgar Erice on Monday said they are both ready to face the defamation charges.
Trillanes said the vice president just wanted to scare his detractors by filing the damage suit.
“
Nagpapakita lamang ito na nagpapanic na si VP Binay kaya sinusubukan na lang niyang takutin ang mga taong umuusig sa kanya
[This only hows that VP Binay is panicking so he's trying to scare his accusers],” Trillanes said.
“Nonetheless, we will face the charges in court and will continue exposing his misdeeds," Trillanes said in Filipino.
For his part, Erice said: “It's a privilege. I am willing to suffer the consequences of fighting and exposing a trapo who did much harm to Makati and our country.”
He also mocked Binay for allegedly not being "happy with his millions" and wanting to fleec them for more to "use for his campaign."
Ateneo Law School dean Antonio La Vina said Binay's case "has no future" and was "for harassment purposes." He added that filing the suit might even hurt the Vice President by showing him up as "vindictive" and distracting him "from talking about his visions for the country."

Basis of the suit
Binay said the basis of his suit against the defendants was the sheer malice, ill will and spite that fueled the efforts to discredit him and thus weaken, if not destroy, his chances of winning in the May 2016 Elections.
“They were not made in response to duty but with the obvious intention to injure the reputation of an innocent person in order to attain their objectives. Worse, through the fault and/or negligence of the Defendants, the false imputations against the Vice President were clearly predicated on erroneous findings as the imputations, among other things, disregarded more than 3 decades of public documents. Worst, they were done in total breach and utter disregard of the Vice President’s rights, including but not limited to the right to due process,” Binay argued.
“As such, the Defendants are liable for their acts and omissions that caused the defamation of the name and reputation of the Vice President,” he added.
Binay said that the defendants are likewise jointly and solidarily liable for their acts and omissions pursuant to Articles 20, 21 and 2176 of the Civil Code.
“Moreover, in view of said acts, the Defendants wilfully and/or negligently caused damage, loss and injury upon the Vice President in a manner that is clearly contrary to law, morals, good customs and public policy,” Binay added.
Binay said that even assuming arguendo that the defendants had the right to make the defamatory statements and/or caused the undue publication thereof, the exercise of said right by the Defendants still constitute an abuse of right under Article 19 of the Civil Code.
“Resultantly, the Defendants are still liable for damages,” Binay said.
Binay said that alternatively or cumulatively, by publicly and maliciously imputing to the Vice President, in furtherance of their grand and malevolent orchestrated persecution, the commission of the crimes of graft and corruption and other illegal activities such as amassing ill-gotten wealth, in order to cause dishonor, discredit or contempt to the Vice President, “the defendants are civily liable for defamation under Articles 33 of the Civil Code in relation to Articles 353, 355 and 358 of the Revised Penal Code.”
