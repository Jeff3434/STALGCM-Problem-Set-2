Erap not interested in running for VP
By Jose Rodel Clapano
(The Philippine Star)
|
Updated June 7, 2015 - 12:00am
Former President and now Manila Mayor Joseph Estrada
MANILA, Philippines - Former President and now Manila Mayor Joseph Estrada declared he has no intention to run for vice president in the 2016 elections.
Estrada said his plan is to run for reelection as Manila mayor.
“Not anymore. I’ve been there already. It is not acceptable to me. Not anymore, I guess,” Estrada said over TV5 on Friday.
Estrada made the comment following the statement of Vice President Jejomar Binay that the best tandem for the 2016 presidential elections would be him and the former president.
Estrada, however, stressed he wants to run for reelection in Manila.
He admitted during an interview with GMA-7 on Friday that he already talked to Binay.

“I said don’t lose hope. That is politics. Negative things just come up every single day,” he said.
Binay has been under fire, particularly on social media, following his involvement in the alleged anomalous construction of the Makati City Hall building 2 that he has denied.
His camp also got entangled in a word war with Sen. Grace Poe after United Nationalist Alliance (UNA) president Navotas Rep. Toby Tiangco claimed the lady senator is not qualified to run for president for lack of the ten-year residency requirement.
Despite the consistent demolition job against Binay, he remained on top on poll surveys among the people who expressed their intentions to run for president in 2016.
Estrada, for his part, has kept his options open. He earlier said that he would be forced to run as the opposition standard-bearer should it failed to field a candidate.
Estrada though maintained Binay is still his presidential candidate in 2016.
In 2010, Estrada ran for president under UNA with Binay as his running mate.
Estrada lost to incumbent President Aquino while Binay won over Manuel Roxas II, Aquino’s running mate.
“My focus as of now is bringing back Manila to its old glory as a progressive capital city of the country,” Estrada said.
Binay himself floated the possible return of his tandem with Estrada during a visit to Biliran on Friday.
“It will no longer be Estrada-Binay, it will be Binay-Estrada or BEST. BEST means Binay-Estrada. It’s going to be Binay-Estrada. That is the BEST,” he said.
