PDP-Laban bolts UNA after Binay resigns as chairman
By:
Ernie Reyes, InterAksyon.com
March 7, 2014 1:47 PM
Tweet
InterAksyon.com
The online news portal of TV5
MANILA, Philippines -- A week after Vice President Jejomar Binay said he was resigning as its chairman, the
Partido ng Demokratikong Pilipino-Laban ng Bayan
withdrew from the United Nationalist Alliance, the coalition formed for the 2013 elections with the
Puwersa ng Masang Pilipino.
In a statement, PDP-Laban spokesman Doy del Castillo thanked Binay for his services to the party and wished him well in his bid to seek the presidency in 2016, which the Vice President said he intended to do under a new party he is forming.
Binay had been a stalwart of the PDP-Laban since 1983, when it was formed through the merger of then Cagayan de Oro Mayor Aquilino “Nene” Pimentel Jr.’s PDP and Laban of the late Senator Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino Jr.
Binay said he left the PDP-Laban over differences with its leaders, particularly Senator Aquilino "Koko" Pimentel III, who ran with the administration coalition in last year’s elections after his nemesis, former Senator Juan Miguel Zubiri, was accepted into UNA’s slate.
Zubiri resigned from the Senate in 2011 over accusations he had earned his seat because of fraud in the 2007 polls at the expense of Pimentel III, who then took over his post.
Despite Binay’s resignation, Pimentel III said this was not official yet since the party had not been formally notified. He also refused to comment on Binay’s presidential plans.
Meanwhile, Del Castillo said the PDP-Laban’s leadership will meet on March 13, the party’s 32nd founding anniversary, to discuss the new developments and attend a symposium on local fiscal autonomy, a primary initiative of the party.
Del Castillo said PDP Laban wants to give the youth a bigger role in the programs and projects of the party along with its thrusts to strengthen local governments through genuine fiscal autonomy and to establish a federal system of government.
Among the speakers at the event, to be held at the Makati Palace Hotel, are Bataan Representative Enrique Garcia and former Batangas Representative Hermilando Mandanas.
The elder Pimentel, father of the Local Government Code, will give insights on needed amendments to the Code.
